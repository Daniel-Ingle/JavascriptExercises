"Recursion" is a problem-solving technique (aka, 'algorithm') where the solution depends on solutions to smaller instances of the SAME problem. In programming, it is often just known as a function which calls itself.

Recursive functions need a "base case," a condition which terminates the recursion. This prevents an "infinite loop."

NOTES:

  • Though it may solve the problem, it may not be "faster" to run.
  • Recursion might be hard to understand at first, particuarly for bigger problems.

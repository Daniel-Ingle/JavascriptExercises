Sorting: Insertion Sort


Given an array of 'n' elements, we need to sort the elements. For our cities in Montana, we want to sort them alphabetically.

We'll do this using a Bubble Sort algorithm.

It works by comparing the city at index 0 with the city at index 1 and, if the city at index 1 should come before, it swaps those two elements. Then, the city now at index 1 is compared with the city at index 2, and again index 1 and index 2 are swapped if 2 should come before 1. And on through to index array.length - 1.

But, we need to iterate again, this time only going through to the next-to-last element in the array, since we will have already "pushed" last city to the end. So the next iteration only goes to array.length - 1 - 1. And we do it again, up to array.length - 2 - 1, and again, and again, all the way up to array.length - array.length - 1.
